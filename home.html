About our Company

We are a dedicated group of innovators, delivering multifunction airframe, high-quality ESC, and GCS and boundless creativity through precision design and advanced coding.

Over Current

The UAV's Overcurrent Alert feature utilizes ACS sensor and Arduino IDE to detect and promptly alert users when current exceeds the limit, ensuring safety and preventing damage.

Flight Time Estimation

The Flight Time Estimation feature on UAV enables real-time monitoring of power consumption, ensuring precise control and reliable information for efficient flight planning and management.

Over Heat

The UAV's Overheat Protection feature utilizes a DS18B20 temperature sensor on the MOSFET to detect temperatures. If it exceeds 70°C, the feature activates the fan, ensuring optimal performance.

Antenna Tracker

The Antenna Tracker is a cutting-edge device designed to automatically track and align antennas with moving targets, ensuring optimal signal reception and transmission. Ideal for use in applications such as UAV (unmanned aerial vehicle) communications, satellite ground stations, and mobile broadcast units, this sophisticated system enhances connectivity and data transfer efficiency.

Battery Percentage

ESC's Battery Percentage feature utilizes a voltage sensor to accurately measure battery power. By mapping the voltage data to a desired percentage range using Arduino IDE's map function, the application displays real-time ESC power usage.

Online Monitoring

The UAV's Online Monitoring feature enables real-time surveillance through a user-friendly website, providing remote access to data such as position, battery status, sensor data, and performance visualizations.

GCS Application

The Ground Control Station (GCS) application is a robust and versatile software solution designed for the remote management and operation of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). It serves as the central interface for monitoring, controlling, and configuring UAV missions, providing users with real-time data and comprehensive control over their aerial assets.

Flexibility

To support flexibility in use, the UAV design is designed to be easily configured in three flight modes: high-wing, quadcopter, and low-wing. The purpose of each of these modes is to provide adaptability in a variety of flight scenarios, from high stability in the high-wing to more agile maneuvers in the quadcopter and low-wing.
